Engaging listeners with binaural audiobooks

Despite their recent popularity, next to no audiobooks utilise binaural audio (3D audio in essence), leaving the story no more immersive than that of a regular book. Therefore this project implemented a binaural audiobook creation tool. This tool allows creators to make binaural audio with the use of the visual spatial circles. It is as simple as pressing record and then putting the red cirle where you want the audio to come from.

Technical details

It was found that in natural spatial hearing, interaural and dynamic cues are used for directional location. To implement the interaural cues, sound has to be either recorded using a dummy head or by modifying the audio using the impulse responses from such a head. Pre-made HRIR's (Head Related Impulse Responses) made it possible to synthesise binaural audio. A gyroscope made it possible to move the binaural audio around the listeners head, to simulate the dynamic cue. This project was a 5th semester university project from AAU which was praised by the examiner and censor.
